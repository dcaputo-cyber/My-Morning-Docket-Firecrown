*DEEP DIVE — COPPA Rule amendments + age-verification policy statement*

1. *Architecture.* First amendment cycle since 2013. Effective April 22, 2026, on a one-year runway from the April 2025 final-rule publication. Applicability test unchanged: operators of services directed to children under 13, plus actual-knowledge operators. What changed is what the rule requires.

2. *Four operational shifts.* (a) Strict data retention — only "reasonably necessary" for a "specific purpose," indefinite retention prohibited; (b) expanded PII definition adds biometric identifiers (voiceprints, gait, facial geometry) and government-issued IDs; (c) separate VPC for third-party disclosure for targeted advertising — single-consent flows are now non-compliant; (d) Safe Harbor transparency — public membership lists, triennial tech reports, annual disciplinary disclosures.

3. *The age-verification safe harbor.* Feb 10 policy statement: FTC will not bring COPPA actions against operators using personal info "solely" for age verification, provided no retention beyond what's necessary, no third-party disclosure for other purposes. This removes the federal-level deterrent on age-gating that had been keeping deferred age-gate projects in the engineering backlog. State law still applies (MD's 18-yr line, CT's 16-yr line, EU age-assurance posture).
